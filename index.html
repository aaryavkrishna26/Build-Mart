🏗️ BuildMart

A hyperlocal construction materials marketplace — connecting buyers and sellers across Indian cities in real time.

ID: 2427030754  ·  2427030767  ·  2427030765

Problem Statement

The procurement of construction materials in Indian Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities is largely unorganized. Buyers — individual homeowners, contractors, and small builders — have no reliable digital platform to discover local sellers, compare prices, or place orders online. They depend on phone calls and physical visits to multiple shops, which wastes time and money.

Existing platforms such as IndiaMart and OLX are either too expensive for small sellers or too general-purpose to be useful for construction material procurement at a local level. There is an urgent need for a free, focused, location-aware marketplace that brings local construction material sellers and buyers together in one place.

Literature Review / Market Research

Overview of Existing Platforms

Several platforms exist for B2B and B2C product discovery in India, but none specifically address the construction materials buying process at a local level. IndiaMart and TradeIndia are the most widely used platforms for industrial products in India. However, both require paid subscriptions for sellers to get buyer leads, making them inaccessible to small local dealers. Buyers on these platforms cannot compare prices across sellers in real time and must wait for sellers to respond via inquiry forms, which introduces delays in the procurement process.

OLX allows free listings but is a general classifieds platform with no category structure for construction materials, no price comparison feature, no cart system, and no buyer-seller trust mechanism. Amazon Business offers a structured buying experience with cart and checkout but is not designed for local construction material dealers — it is largely unsuitable for hyperlocal procurement where buyers need material from nearby suppliers to minimize transport cost.

Academic studies have noted that a large percentage of small construction buyers in Indian Tier-2 cities still rely on phone calls and physical visits to compare prices among local dealers, showing a clear gap between existing digital platforms and the actual needs of this market.

Research Gap / Innovation

The Gap

Existing platforms such as IndiaMart, TradeIndia, and OLX do not provide a location-first, category-driven workflow that guides buyers from city selection to material selection to seller comparison in a single flow. This is the core gap that BuildMart addresses.

No existing construction material platform in India combines all five innovations below in a single free-to-use web application targeted at Tier-2 and Tier-3 city markets.

Five Key Innovations in BuildMart

1
NEWHyperlocal discovery flow — buyers first select a city, then a material category, before seeing any listings. No existing platform guides users this way.
2
NEWReal-time price comparison catalog — multiple sellers are shown side by side with price, quantity, and contact details on one page.
3
NEWFree seller shop profile — supports GST number, shop bio, area address, and contact details. No subscription or lead fee required.
4
NEWIntegrated cart & checkout — full e-commerce flow with Cash on Delivery, UPI, and card payment options, tailored for construction material orders.
5
NEWSeller-side order dashboard — local dealers can confirm, dispatch, and mark orders as delivered without any third-party logistics tool.

System Methodology

Dataset / Input

Seed data includes 25+ pre-configured sellers across 5 major Indian cities — Mumbai, Delhi, Lucknow, Jaipur, and Indore — with city-specific pricing multipliers. Material categories: Cement, Sand, Steel/TMT Bars, Bricks, Aggregates, Paint, Tiles, Pipes, Electrical, and Wood/Timber.

User inputs: city selection, material category, cart items, delivery address, and payment method. All inputs are validated on both frontend (React) and backend (Express + Mongoose).

Model / Architecture

BuildMart uses a three-layer MERN stack architecture:

F
Frontend — React.js — Component-based UI with Context API for cart and location state. JWT tokens stored in localStorage.
B
Backend — Node.js + Express — REST API with 7 route modules: auth, materials, cart, orders, coupons, profile, sellerProfile. JWT middleware for protected routes.
D
Database — MongoDB + Mongoose — 5 models: User (buyer/seller roles), Material, Cart, Order, Coupon. Location and category fields on every material document.
